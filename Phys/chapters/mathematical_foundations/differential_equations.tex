\section{Simple differential equations}

A differential equation is an equation involving an unknown function and one or more of its derivatives. It describes a relation between a function and its change.

\begin{definitionbox}
A differential equation does not ask for a number. It asks for a function.
\end{definitionbox}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
Differential equation & General solution & Check \\
\midrule
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx}=3\) & \(y(x)=3x+C\) & derivative is \(3\) \\
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx}=2x\) & \(y(x)=x^2+C\) & derivative is \(2x\) \\
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx}=ky\) & \(y(x)=Ce^{kx}\) & derivative is \(kCe^{kx}\) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The constant \(C\) is determined by an additional condition. For example, if
\[
y(0)=2,
\]
and
\[
y(x)=Ce^{kx},
\]
then
\[
2=Ce^{k\cdot 0}=C,
\]
so
\[
y(x)=2e^{kx}.
\]

\begin{remarkbox}
Here the important point is only the basic structure: a differential equation contains derivatives and asks for an unknown function.
\end{remarkbox}
