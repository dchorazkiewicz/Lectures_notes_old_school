## 2. Vectors, Bases, and Coordinate Systems

### Vectors from coordinate displacements

- Vector addition as composition of displacements.
- Scalar multiplication as repetition, reversal, and rescaling.
- Linear combinations of vectors.
- Length and inclination of a vector.
- Parallelism and perpendicularity.
- The dot product and its properties.
- The angle between vectors.
- The cosine theorem and its relation to the dot product.
- Projection of one vector onto another.
- Orthogonal decomposition.
- Extension of vectors and coordinates to three dimensions.

### Changing the basis

- A basis as a choice of coordinate directions.
- Coordinates of a fixed geometric vector in different bases.
- Change-of-basis calculations.
- Examples in which coordinates in a new basis become negative.

### Nonlinear coordinate systems

- Polar coordinates.
- Describing the same point using distance and direction instead of Cartesian components.
