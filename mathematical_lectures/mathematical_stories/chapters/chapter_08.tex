\chapter{Systems of Linear Equations}

\begin{corebox}[Several conditions, one solution set]
A linear system asks for values satisfying all equations simultaneously. Matrices organise the coefficients, geometry interprets the common solution set, and elimination, determinants, and inverse matrices provide complementary viewpoints.
\end{corebox}

\begin{summarybox}[Systems of linear equations]
A system may have one solution, no solution, or infinitely many solutions. Gaussian elimination is the general classification method. For square systems with nonzero determinant, Cramer's rule and the inverse formula give two additional descriptions of the same unique solution. Singular and parametric cases return to compatibility, pivots, and free variables.
\end{summarybox}
