\chapter{Matrix Inversion}

\begin{corebox}[Reversing a matrix action]
An inverse matrix is not a formal fraction. It is a matrix that reconstructs the original input from the output of an invertible matrix. Determinants test whether reversal is possible; elimination, explicit formulas, and cofactors construct the reversing matrix.
\end{corebox}

\begin{summarybox}[Matrix inversion]
A square matrix is invertible exactly when its determinant is nonzero. Inverses may be found by the $2\times2$ formula, Gauss--Jordan elimination, the adjugate formula, or visible structural identities. They solve equations by cancelling matrix actions on the correct side and in the correct order.
\end{summarybox}
