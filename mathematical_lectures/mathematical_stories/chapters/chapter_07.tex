\chapter{Matrix Inversion}

\begin{corebox}[Purpose and learning goals]
This chapter studies when a matrix action can be reversed and how the reversing matrix is constructed. After completing it, the student should be able to:
\begin{itemize}
\item explain the meaning of an inverse and the role of a nonzero determinant;
\item compute inverses in elementary cases, especially by the $2\times2$ formula and Gauss--Jordan elimination;
\item use inverses in matrix equations while respecting multiplication order.
\end{itemize}
\end{corebox}

\begin{summarybox}[Matrix inversion]
A square matrix is invertible exactly when its determinant is nonzero. The $2\times2$ formula gives the first explicit case, Gauss--Jordan is the main general hand method, and the adjugate formula explains the determinant--cofactor connection. Structural identities and matrix equations show how inverses cancel actions on the correct side and in the correct order.
\end{summarybox}
