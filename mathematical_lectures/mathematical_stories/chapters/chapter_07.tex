\noindent\textbf{Matrix inversion.} A square matrix is invertible exactly when its determinant is nonzero. The $2\times2$ formula gives the first explicit case, Gauss--Jordan is the main general hand method, and the adjugate formula explains the determinant--cofactor connection. Structural identities and matrix equations show how inverses cancel actions on the correct side and in the correct order.
