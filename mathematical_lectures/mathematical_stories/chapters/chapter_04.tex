\noindent\textbf{From curves to surfaces.} A circle, parabola, ellipse, or hyperbola is first determined by a geometric condition and then represented by an equation. Completing squares and rotating coordinates recover that geometry from a general quadratic equation. In three dimensions, families of planar sections reveal whether a quadric is bounded, connected, separated, cylindrical, conical, or saddle-shaped.
