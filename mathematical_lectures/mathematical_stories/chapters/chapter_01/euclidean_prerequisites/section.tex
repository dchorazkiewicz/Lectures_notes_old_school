\section{Brief Euclidean Background}

Before introducing coordinates, we recall the geometric objects that the Cartesian construction will describe. This is background and reference material rather than the main computational subject of the chapter.

\begin{corebox}[What must be retained]
Points, straight lines, circles, right angles, parallelism, congruence, and elementary constructions are meaningful before coordinates. The Cartesian system adds an algebraic language to this geometry; it does not create the geometry itself.
\end{corebox}
