\section{Brief Euclidean Background}

Before introducing coordinates, we recall only the geometric ingredients that the Cartesian construction requires.

\begin{corebox}[What is available before coordinates]
Points, straight lines, right angles, lengths, and perpendicular projection are already meaningful geometrically. A Cartesian system does not create these objects; it assigns numerical descriptions to them after axes, an origin, directions, and a unit have been chosen.
\end{corebox}

The detailed postulates and construction rules are collected later as a self-study reference. The main narrative now proceeds directly to the Cartesian plane.
