\noindent\textbf{Cartesian space.} The Cartesian plane is produced by explicit choices: two perpendicular axes, their intersection as origin, positive orientations, and a common unit. Orthogonal projection assigns an ordered pair $(x,y)$ to every point. Equations then select geometric sets of points, intersections impose several conditions simultaneously, and the distance formula translates Euclidean length into arithmetic.
