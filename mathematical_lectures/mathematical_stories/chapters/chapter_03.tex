\chapter{Lines and Planes in the Plane and in Space}

This chapter studies lines and planes as geometric sets described in several equivalent ways. After completing it, the student should be able to:
\begin{itemize}
\item construct a line or plane from points and allowed directions;
\item move between parametric, normal, implicit, and slope descriptions;
\item choose the form that best reveals intersections, parallelism, or perpendicularity.
\end{itemize}
