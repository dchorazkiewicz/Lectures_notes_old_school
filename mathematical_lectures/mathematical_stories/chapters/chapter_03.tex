\chapter{Lines and Planes in the Plane and in Space}

\begin{corebox}[The organising question]
Lines and planes are geometric sets generated by allowed directions of motion.
One point and one nonzero direction generate a line; one point and two
independent directions generate a plane. Parametric, normal, implicit, and slope
forms are different descriptions of those same sets, chosen to answer different
questions.
\end{corebox}
