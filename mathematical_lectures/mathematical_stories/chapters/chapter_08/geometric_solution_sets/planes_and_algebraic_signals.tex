\subsection{Planes in Space and Algebraic Signals}

In three variables, an equation
\[
ax+by+cz=d
\]
usually describes a plane. Several planes may meet at one point, share a line, coincide, or have no common point.

\begin{corebox}[The classification is unchanged]
The geometry is richer in space, but the algebraic outcomes remain: exactly one solution, no solution, or infinitely many solutions.
\end{corebox}

During elimination,
\[
0=5
\]
signals inconsistency, while
\[
0=0
\]
signals a redundant condition. Proportional left-hand sides may represent parallel or identical geometric objects depending on the right-hand sides.

\begin{summarybox}[Geometry explains elimination]
Algebraic row patterns are not arbitrary symbols. They record whether the geometric conditions intersect, conflict, or repeat one another.
\end{summarybox}
