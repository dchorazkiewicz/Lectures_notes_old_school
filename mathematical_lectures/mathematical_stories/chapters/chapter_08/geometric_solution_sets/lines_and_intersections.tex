\subsection{Lines in the Plane and Their Intersections}

In two variables, a linear equation
\[
ax+by=c
\]
usually describes a line. A system of two equations asks for points lying on both lines.

\begin{examplebox}
For
\[
\begin{cases}
x+y=5,\\
2x-y=1,
\end{cases}
\]
the point $(2,3)$ lies on both lines. Geometrically, solving the system means finding their common point.
\end{examplebox}

\begin{corebox}[Solution set as intersection]
Each equation defines a geometric set. The solution set of the system is the intersection of all those sets.
\end{corebox}
