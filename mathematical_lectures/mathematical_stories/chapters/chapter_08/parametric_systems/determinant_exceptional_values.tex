\subsection{Using the Determinant to Find Exceptional Values}

Let
\[
A(t)=\begin{pmatrix}t&1\\4&t\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Then
\[
\det A(t)=t^2-4=(t-2)(t+2).
\]

\begin{corebox}[Generic and exceptional parameter values]
For $t\neq2,-2$, the matrix is invertible, so every system $A(t)x=b$ has exactly one solution. The values $t=2$ and $t=-2$ must be analysed separately.
\end{corebox}

At an exceptional value, the determinant tells us only that uniqueness is lost. Row reduction or direct equation comparison must decide whether the system is inconsistent or has infinitely many solutions.
