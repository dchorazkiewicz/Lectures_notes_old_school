\subsection{A Classification Strategy and Chapter Synthesis}

\begin{summarybox}[Parameter-system strategy]
For a square system $A(t)x=b(t)$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item compute $\det A(t)$;
\item solve $\det A(t)=0$ to find exceptional values;
\item conclude one solution for all non-exceptional values;
\item analyse every exceptional value separately by elimination or equation comparison;
\item classify each case as one solution, no solution, or infinitely many solutions.
\end{enumerate}
\end{summarybox}

Matrices organise coefficients, row operations simplify simultaneous conditions, determinants locate possible loss of uniqueness, inverse matrices solve reversible square systems, and Cramer's rule expresses their unique solutions through determinants.

\begin{corebox}[One object, several methods]
These are not isolated tricks. They are different ways of reading the same system of linear conditions: its compatibility, dependence, freedom, and solution set.
\end{corebox}
