\subsection{The General Square Case}

For a square system
\[
Ax=b,
\]
assume $A\in\mathbb R^{n\times n}$ and $\det A\neq0$. Let $A_i$ be obtained from $A$ by replacing its $i$-th column with $b$.

\begin{theorembox}[Cramer's rule]
The unique solution satisfies
\[
\boxed{x_i=\frac{\det A_i}{\det A}},
\qquad i=1,\ldots,n.
\]
\end{theorembox}

The formula is elegant but requires many determinant calculations. Gaussian elimination is usually more efficient for larger systems.

\begin{interpretationbox}[Why the rule has a denominator]
The coefficient determinant must be nonzero because the equations must contain enough independent information to determine one solution.
\end{interpretationbox}
