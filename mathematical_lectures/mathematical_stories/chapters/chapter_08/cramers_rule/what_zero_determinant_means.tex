\subsection{What a Zero Coefficient Determinant Means}

If $\det A=0$, Cramer's rule does not apply. This does not automatically mean that the system is inconsistent; it means only that a unique solution cannot be obtained from an invertible coefficient matrix.

\begin{examplebox}
For
\[
\begin{cases}
x+y=2,\\
2x+2y=4,
\end{cases}
\]
the coefficient determinant is zero, but the second equation repeats the first. The system has infinitely many solutions.
\end{examplebox}

\begin{corebox}[Loss of uniqueness is not loss of existence]
When $\det A=0$, further elimination or equation comparison is required to distinguish no solution from infinitely many solutions.
\end{corebox}

\begin{summarybox}[Using Cramer's rule responsibly]
Check that the system is square, compute $\det A$, replace the correct column for each unknown, verify the answer, and classify singular cases separately.
\end{summarybox}
