\subsection{A Worked Cramer Example}

Solve
\[
\begin{cases}
2x+y=5,\\
x-y=1.
\end{cases}
\]
The coefficient matrix is
\[
A=\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&-1\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\det A=-3.
\]

\begin{derivationbox}[Replace one column at a time]
For $x$,
\[
A_x=\begin{pmatrix}5&1\\1&-1\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\det A_x=-6,
\]
so
\[
x=\frac{-6}{-3}=2.
\]
For $y$,
\[
A_y=\begin{pmatrix}2&5\\1&1\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\det A_y=-3,
\]
so
\[
y=\frac{-3}{-3}=1.
\]
\end{derivationbox}

Thus
\[
\boxed{(x,y)=(2,1)}.
\]
Substitution verifies both original equations.
