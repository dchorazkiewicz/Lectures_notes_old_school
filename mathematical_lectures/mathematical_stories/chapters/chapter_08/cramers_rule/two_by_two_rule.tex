\subsection{The $2\times2$ Rule}

For
\[
\begin{cases}
ax+by=e,\\
cx+dy=f,
\end{cases}
\qquad
A=\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix},
\]
assume $\det A\neq0$. Replace one coefficient column at a time by the right-hand side:
\[
A_x=\begin{pmatrix}e&b\\f&d\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
A_y=\begin{pmatrix}a&e\\c&f\end{pmatrix}.
\]

\begin{theorembox}[Cramer's rule in dimension two]
\[
\boxed{x=\frac{\det A_x}{\det A},
\qquad
y=\frac{\det A_y}{\det A}}.
\]
\end{theorembox}

\begin{remarkbox}
The denominator condition is structural. If $\det A=0$, the coefficient matrix is singular and Cramer's rule does not apply.
\end{remarkbox}
