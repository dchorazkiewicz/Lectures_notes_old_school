\subsection{Classification After Row Reduction}

\begin{summarybox}[Three diagnostic patterns]
After reduction:
\begin{itemize}
\item a pivot in every variable column and no contradiction gives exactly one solution;
\item a row $0=\text{nonzero}$ gives no solution;
\item at least one free variable and no contradiction gives infinitely many solutions.
\end{itemize}
\end{summarybox}

Gaussian elimination is the most flexible method in this chapter. It applies to square and rectangular systems and reveals both the solution and the structure of the solution set.
