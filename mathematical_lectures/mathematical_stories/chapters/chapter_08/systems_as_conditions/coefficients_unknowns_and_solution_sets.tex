\subsection{Unknowns, Coefficients, Constants, and Solution Sets}

A linear equation in $x_1,\ldots,x_n$ has the form
\[
a_1x_1+\cdots+a_nx_n=b.
\]
The numbers $a_i$ are coefficients and $b$ is the right-hand side.

A system with $m$ equations and $n$ unknowns has the form
\[
\begin{cases}
a_{11}x_1+\cdots+a_{1n}x_n=b_1,\\
\vdots\\
a_{m1}x_1+\cdots+a_{mn}x_n=b_m.
\end{cases}
\]

\begin{definitionbox}
The solution set is the set of all assignments satisfying every equation. For a linear system, it may contain exactly one point, no points, or infinitely many points.
\end{definitionbox}

\begin{remarkbox}
The coefficients describe how the unknowns enter the conditions; the right-hand sides specify the required values of those linear combinations.
\end{remarkbox}
