\section*{The Intellectual Roadmap of the Course}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{The Intellectual Roadmap of the Course}

The chapters of this course are not intended to be independent containers of
facts. They form one developing argument about how mathematical ideas are born.
A new object appears because an earlier language is no longer sufficient for a
question we want to ask. A formula appears because a geometric or algebraic
requirement has been translated into symbols. A new method becomes necessary
because an older representation hides the structure that matters.

For this reason, the order of the material is part of the mathematics. We begin
with geometry before coordinates, construct a numerical language for that
geometry, introduce vectors when positions alone no longer describe change, and
use those vectors to construct lines and planes. We then replace restrictions on
direction by conditions on distance, producing conic sections and quadric
surfaces.

The roadmap below is not another list of formulas. It states what each developed
chapter is trying to make visible, which questions motivate its sections, and
what kind of mathematical independence the student should acquire. The desired
result is not merely that a calculation can be repeated, but that the student can
explain why it is appropriate, what object it describes, which assumptions are
being used, and how the result should be interpreted.
