\chapter{Derivatives and Function Analysis}

\begin{corebox}[Purpose and learning goals]
This chapter develops the derivative as a measure of local change and uses it to read global behaviour. After completing it, the student should be able to:
\begin{itemize}
\item connect difference quotients, tangent slopes, and the derivative;
\item differentiate elementary functions and interpret derivative units and signs;
\item analyse monotonicity, extrema, graph shape, motion, and simple optimisation models.
\end{itemize}
\end{corebox}

\begin{remarkbox}[Lecture emphasis]
The live core is the derivative from a limiting secant, elementary rules, tangent interpretation, monotonicity, extrema, and one model of motion or optimisation. Second-derivative analysis, growth models, and a complete function study provide a fuller written continuation.
\end{remarkbox}

\begin{summarybox}[Derivatives in analysis and applications]
A derivative connects a local limit with tangent geometry, units, motion, growth, graph shape, and optimal design. The mean value theorem supplies the bridge from local derivative signs to monotonicity on an interval. The central skill is translating derivative information into conclusions while keeping the model, domain, and interpretation visible.
\end{summarybox}
