\chapter{Derivatives and Function Analysis}

\begin{corebox}[Simple calculations, rich interpretation]
The derivative is introduced as a limit that measures local change. Elementary formulas are sufficient to study tangent slopes, motion, monotonicity, extrema, convexity, optimisation, and simple growth models.
\end{corebox}

\begin{summarybox}[Derivatives in analysis and applications]
A derivative connects a local calculation with a statement about change. The central skill is not manipulating complicated expressions but translating the sign and value of derivatives into conclusions about a graph, motion, an optimum, or a simple model.
\end{summarybox}
