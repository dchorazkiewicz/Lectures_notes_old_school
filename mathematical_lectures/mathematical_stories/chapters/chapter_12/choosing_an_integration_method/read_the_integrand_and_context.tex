\subsection{Read the integrand and the modelling question}

\begin{corebox}[Method follows structure]
First simplify the integrand. Then ask whether it is an elementary antiderivative, a direct substitution pattern, or a simple product suited to integration by parts. For a definite integral, also interpret the interval, sign, and units.
\end{corebox}

\begin{examplebox}[Rewrite before integrating]
\[
\int \frac{\sqrt{x}}{x^2}\,dx
=\int x^{-3/2}\,dx
=-2x^{-1/2}+C.
\]
No advanced method is needed after the expression is rewritten clearly.
\end{examplebox}

\begin{summarybox}[A deliberately limited toolkit]
Use basic antiderivatives for powers, exponentials, sine, and cosine; direct substitution when an inner derivative is visible; and integration by parts only for elementary products such as $xe^x$. Use definite integrals to express area, accumulated change, displacement, and work. Verify indefinite integrals by differentiation and check signs, scale, units, and restrictions in applications.
\end{summarybox}

The purpose of the chapter is to understand integration as accumulation and reversal of differentiation. Difficult rational decompositions and elaborate trigonometric techniques are outside the intended scope.
