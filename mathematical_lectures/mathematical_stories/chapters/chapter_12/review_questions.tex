\section{Review Questions}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Distinguish an antiderivative from a definite integral. Why is the first answer a family of functions while the second is a number?

\item Explain the role of the constant of integration. What information is lost when a function is differentiated?

\item Find several elementary antiderivatives and verify each answer by differentiation. Explain why verification is especially natural for indefinite integration.

\item Describe substitution as the reversal of the chain rule. In a simple example, identify the inner function, its derivative, and the new variable.

\item Derive integration by parts from the product rule and use it in one elementary example. What structural choice determines which factor is differentiated?

\item Explain how a Riemann sum approximates accumulated change. Identify the meanings of the partition, subinterval width, sample point, and local contribution.

\item State both parts of the fundamental theorem of calculus. Explain why this theorem is the bridge between a limit of sums and antiderivatives.

\item Compare signed area with total geometric area. Give an example in which cancellation makes the definite integral zero although the graph encloses positive geometric area.

\item Formulate each of the following as an integral of a local contribution:
\begin{enumerate}
\item area between two curves;
\item volume by cross-sectional areas;
\item displacement and total distance from velocity;
\item work from a variable force.
\end{enumerate}
State what the integrand and differential represent in each model.

\item A quantity $Q$ changes at rate $Q'(t)$. Explain and apply the principle
\[
Q(b)-Q(a)=\int_a^b Q'(t)\,dt.
\]
Why does this one statement unify many apparently different applications of integration?
\end{enumerate}
