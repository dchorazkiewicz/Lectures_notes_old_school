\section{Review Exercises}

\subsection{Elementary computational exercises}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Find
\[
\int(3x^2-4x+5)\,dx,
\qquad
\int(2e^x+3\cos x)\,dx,
\qquad
\int x^{-1/2}\,dx.
\]
Differentiate each answer.

\item Find $F$ satisfying
\[
F'(x)=4x-3,
\qquad F(2)=5,
\]
and compute $F(0)$.

\item Evaluate by substitution:
\[
\int 2x(x^2+1)^3\,dx,
\qquad
\int_0^1 2xe^{x^2}\,dx.
\]

\item Evaluate by parts:
\[
\int xe^x\,dx,
\qquad
\int_0^1 xe^x\,dx.
\]

\item For $f(x)=x$ on $[0,2]$, compute right-endpoint Riemann sums for $n=2$ and $n=4$, then compare them with the exact integral.

\item Evaluate
\[
\int_0^3(2x+1)\,dx,
\qquad
\int_{-1}^{2}(x^2-1)\,dx
\]
using the fundamental theorem of calculus.

\item Compute the area under $y=x^2$ on $[0,2]$ and draw the region.

\item Find the average value of $f(x)=x^2$ on $[0,3]$ and a point where the function attains that value.

\item A particle has velocity $v(t)=t-1$ on $[0,3]$. Compute displacement and total distance, splitting at the sign change.

\item A force $F(x)=2x+1$ acts from $x=0$ to $x=3$. Compute the work and state its units when force is in newtons and distance in metres.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Development and reasoning exercises}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Evaluate without being told the method:
\[
\int x(x^2+4)^5\,dx,
\qquad
\int x\cos x\,dx,
\qquad
\int(3x^2+2e^x)\,dx.
\]
For each integral, justify the method before calculating.

\item Find the intersection points of
\[
y=x
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
y=x^2-2x,
\]
determine which graph is above on each interval, and compute the enclosed area.

\item A particle has velocity
\[
v(t)=(t-1)(t-3),
\qquad0\le t\le4.
\]
Compute displacement and total distance. Explain why the interval must be divided at both zeros.

\item The region under $y=\sqrt{x}$ on $[0,4]$ is rotated about the $x$-axis. Build the cross-sectional area and compute the volume.

\item A quantity enters a system at rate $r(t)=3t^2+2$ units per minute for $0\le t\le2$. Find the accumulated amount and explain the units of the integrand, differential, and integral.

\item Define
\[
A(x)=\int_0^x(t^2+1)\,dt.
\]
Find $A(x)$ and $A'(x)$, then explain geometrically why the derivative recovers the current integrand value.

\item Compare right-endpoint Riemann sums for an increasing function with the exact integral. Use $f(x)=x^2$ on $[0,1]$ and explain why the sums overestimate.

\item Construct an example where the definite integral is zero but the total geometric area is positive. Compute both quantities and explain the cancellation.

\item Show explicitly that substitution reverses the chain rule and integration by parts reverses the product rule. Derive both formulas and attach one example to each.

\item Give a complete modelling strategy for accumulated quantities: identify the local contribution and units, choose the interval, determine whether signs or intersections require subdivision, select a method, compute, and interpret the result. Apply it to area, motion, or work.
\end{enumerate}
