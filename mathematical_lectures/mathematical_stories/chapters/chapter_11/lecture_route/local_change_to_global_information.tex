\subsection{From a local limit to global information}

\begin{corebox}[First 45 minutes: derivative as local change]
Build the derivative from average rate of change, a shrinking secant, and a limit. Differentiate a few elementary functions, interpret units, and distinguish continuity from differentiability.
\end{corebox}

\begin{corebox}[Second 45 minutes: using the derivative]
Use signs of $f'$ and $f''$ to study monotonicity, extrema, graph shape, motion, and elementary optimisation. The calculations remain short so that modelling, domains, and interpretation receive most of the attention.
\end{corebox}

Statements turning the sign of a derivative into monotonicity rely on the mean value theorem. The chapter uses this theorem as the bridge from local slopes to behaviour on an interval and states clearly when the full proof is not developed.
