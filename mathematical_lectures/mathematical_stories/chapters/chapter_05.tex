\chapter{Matrices}

\begin{corebox}[From vectors to transformations]
The previous chapters developed geometric objects and their coordinate descriptions. Matrices now provide a compact language for organising several coordinates or equations at once and, later, for describing linear transformations. We begin by learning to read their structure before introducing operations.
\end{corebox}

\begin{summarybox}[Matrices as organised operations]
A matrix has a shape, a positional structure, and a role in a problem. Entrywise operations preserve positions; multiplication combines rows with columns; special patterns reveal simplified behaviour; and row operations expose hidden relations. Row operations will return in the next three chapters with new meanings: tracking determinants, constructing inverses, and preserving solution sets.
\end{summarybox}
