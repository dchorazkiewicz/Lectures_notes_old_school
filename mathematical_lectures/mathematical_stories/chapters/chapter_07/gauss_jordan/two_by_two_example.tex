\subsection{A Complete $2\times2$ Example}

Let
\[
A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2\\3&7\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Start from
\[
\left[\begin{array}{cc|cc}
1&2&1&0\\
3&7&0&1
\end{array}\right].
\]

\begin{derivationbox}[Reduce the left block]
First use
\[
R_2\leftarrow R_2-3R_1,
\]
which gives
\[
\left[\begin{array}{cc|cc}
1&2&1&0\\
0&1&-3&1
\end{array}\right].
\]
Then use
\[
R_1\leftarrow R_1-2R_2,
\]
obtaining
\[
\left[\begin{array}{cc|cc}
1&0&7&-2\\
0&1&-3&1
\end{array}\right].
\]
\end{derivationbox}

Therefore
\[
\boxed{A^{-1}=\begin{pmatrix}7&-2\\-3&1\end{pmatrix}}.
\]
Direct multiplication verifies that $AA^{-1}=I_2$.
