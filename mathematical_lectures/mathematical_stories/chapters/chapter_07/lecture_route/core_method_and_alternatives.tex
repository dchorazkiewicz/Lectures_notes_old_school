\subsection{One main method and two complementary viewpoints}

\begin{corebox}[First 45 minutes: meaning and the $2\times2$ case]
The inverse is introduced as reversal of a matrix action. The determinant answers whether reversal is possible, and the $2\times2$ formula provides the first complete construction.
\end{corebox}

\begin{corebox}[Second 45 minutes: Gauss--Jordan as the main construction]
Gauss--Jordan elimination is the principal hand method because it scales to larger matrices and makes failure informative. The adjugate formula is retained for conceptual value: it explains how cofactors and the determinant combine to build an inverse, but it is not presented as the default algorithm.
\end{corebox}

Structural identities and matrix equations show how inverses are used after they have been found. Every cancellation must occur on the correct side because matrix multiplication is not commutative.
