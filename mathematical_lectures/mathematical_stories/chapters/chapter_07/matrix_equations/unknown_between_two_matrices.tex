\subsection{An Unknown Matrix Between Two Known Matrices}

Suppose
\[
AXB=C
\]
and both $A$ and $B$ are invertible. Undo $A$ on the left and $B$ on the right:
\[
A^{-1}(AXB)B^{-1}=A^{-1}CB^{-1}.
\]
Therefore
\[
\boxed{X=A^{-1}CB^{-1}}.
\]

\begin{interpretationbox}[Cancellation determines the order]
Each inverse must be placed on the side where it becomes adjacent to the matrix it is intended to cancel. The order is a consequence of the original product, not a memorised decoration.
\end{interpretationbox}
