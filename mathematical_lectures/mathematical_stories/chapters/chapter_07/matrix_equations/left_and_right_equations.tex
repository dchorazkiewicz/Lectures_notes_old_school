\subsection{Equations of the Forms $AX=B$ and $XA=B$}

If
\[
AX=B
\]
and $A$ is invertible, multiply on the left:
\[
A^{-1}AX=A^{-1}B,
\qquad
\boxed{X=A^{-1}B}.
\]

If instead
\[
XA=B,
\]
multiply on the right:
\[
XAA^{-1}=BA^{-1},
\qquad
\boxed{X=BA^{-1}}.
\]

\begin{corebox}[The side is determined by the equation]
An inverse cancels a matrix only when it is placed next to that matrix on the correct side. Matrix factors cannot be moved through an equation as if they were ordinary numbers.
\end{corebox}

\begin{examplebox}[Different sides give different answers]
For
\[
A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2\\3&7\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
A^{-1}=\begin{pmatrix}7&-2\\-3&1\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
B=\begin{pmatrix}4&1\\5&2\end{pmatrix},
\]
$AX=B$ gives
\[
X=A^{-1}B=\begin{pmatrix}18&3\\-7&-1\end{pmatrix},
\]
whereas $XA=B$ gives
\[
X=BA^{-1}=\begin{pmatrix}25&-7\\29&-8\end{pmatrix}.
\]
\end{examplebox}
