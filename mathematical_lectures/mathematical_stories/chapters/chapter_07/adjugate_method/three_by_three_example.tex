\subsection{A Complete $3\times3$ Adjugate Example}

Let
\[
A=\begin{pmatrix}
1&0&2\\-1&3&1\\0&2&1
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Expanding along the first row gives
\[
\det A=1\begin{vmatrix}3&1\\2&1\end{vmatrix}
+2\begin{vmatrix}-1&3\\0&2\end{vmatrix}
=1+2(-2)=-3.
\]

\begin{derivationbox}[Cofactors and the adjugate]
The cofactor matrix is
\[
C=\begin{pmatrix}
1&1&-2\\
4&1&-2\\
-6&-3&3
\end{pmatrix},
\]
so
\[
\operatorname{adj}(A)=C^{\mathsf T}
=\begin{pmatrix}
1&4&-6\\
1&1&-3\\
-2&-2&3
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
\end{derivationbox}

Therefore
\[
A^{-1}=\frac1{-3}\operatorname{adj}(A)
=\begin{pmatrix}
-\frac13&-\frac43&2\\
-\frac13&-\frac13&1\\
\frac23&\frac23&-1
\end{pmatrix}.
\]

This method is longer than Gauss--Jordan here, but it displays explicitly how cofactors construct the inverse.
