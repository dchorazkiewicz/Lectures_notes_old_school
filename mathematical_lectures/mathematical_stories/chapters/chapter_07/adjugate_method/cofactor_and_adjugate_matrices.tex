\subsection{The Cofactor Matrix and the Adjugate}

For $A=(a_{ij})$, let
\[
C_{ij}=(-1)^{i+j}M_{ij}
\]
be the cofactor of $a_{ij}$. Arrange all cofactors in the cofactor matrix $C$.

\begin{definitionbox}
The adjugate of $A$ is the transpose of the cofactor matrix:
\[
\boxed{\operatorname{adj}(A)=C^{\mathsf T}}.
\]
\end{definitionbox}

The transpose is essential: cofactors associated with rows become columns in the matrix that reverses the action.

\begin{examplebox}[The construction in dimension two]
For
\[
A=\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix},
\]
the cofactor matrix and adjugate are
\[
C=\begin{pmatrix}d&-c\\-b&a\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\operatorname{adj}(A)=\begin{pmatrix}d&-b\\-c&a\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Thus the familiar $2\times2$ inverse formula is already the adjugate formula in its simplest complete form.
\end{examplebox}
