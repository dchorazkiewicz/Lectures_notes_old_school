\subsection{What the Adjugate Method Is For}

For hand calculations, the adjugate method is natural in the $2\times2$ case because it reproduces the familiar inverse formula immediately. In larger dimensions, computing every cofactor quickly becomes longer than elimination.

\begin{interpretationbox}[Conceptual rather than algorithmic value]
Gauss--Jordan is the principal construction method. The adjugate is retained because it explains why cofactors and the determinant control invertibility and why the condition $\det A\neq0$ appears in every inverse formula.
\end{interpretationbox}

The student should understand the chain
\[
\text{cofactors}\longrightarrow\operatorname{adj}(A)
\longrightarrow A\operatorname{adj}(A)=(\det A)I,
\]
without treating a long cofactor table as the central skill of the chapter.
