\subsection{When the Adjugate Method Is Appropriate}

For hand calculations, the adjugate method is convenient for $2\times2$ matrices and sometimes manageable for $3\times3$ matrices. For larger matrices, computing every cofactor becomes expensive.

\begin{interpretationbox}[Computational and conceptual value]
Gauss--Jordan elimination is often more efficient, but the adjugate method explains why determinants and cofactors govern invertibility.
\end{interpretationbox}

\begin{summarybox}[Adjugate procedure]
Compute $\det A$. If it is nonzero, compute all cofactors, form the cofactor matrix, transpose it, and divide by the determinant.
\end{summarybox}
