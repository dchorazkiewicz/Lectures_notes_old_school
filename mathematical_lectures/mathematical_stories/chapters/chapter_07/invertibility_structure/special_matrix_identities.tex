\subsection{Special Matrix Identities}

Sometimes an identity already contains the inverse.

\begin{examplebox}[An involution]
If
\[
A^2=I,
\]
then $AA=I$, so
\[
\boxed{A^{-1}=A}.
\]
\end{examplebox}

\begin{examplebox}[Extracting an inverse from a polynomial identity]
Suppose
\[
A^2-3A+2I=0.
\]
Then
\[
A(A-3I)=-2I,
\]
so
\[
A\left(-\frac12(A-3I)\right)=I.
\]
Therefore
\[
\boxed{A^{-1}=\frac12(3I-A)}.
\]
\end{examplebox}

\begin{summarybox}[Recognising invertibility]
Inspect diagonal and triangular structure, dependent rows, products of known invertible matrices, and special identities before beginning a long determinant or elimination calculation.
\end{summarybox}
