\subsection{When Does an Inverse Exist?}

\begin{theorembox}[Invertibility criterion]
A square matrix $A$ is invertible if and only if
\[
\boxed{\det A\neq0}.
\]
If $\det A=0$, the matrix is singular and has no inverse.
\end{theorembox}

\begin{interpretationbox}[The determinant as a reversibility test]
A nonzero determinant means that the matrix has not collapsed independent information. A zero determinant signals structural loss and prevents unique reversal.
\end{interpretationbox}

\begin{summarybox}[Questions to ask]
What operation does the matrix perform? Has information been lost? If not, which matrix reverses the operation?
\end{summarybox}
