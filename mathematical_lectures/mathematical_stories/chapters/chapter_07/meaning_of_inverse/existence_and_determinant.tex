\subsection{When Does an Inverse Exist?}

\begin{theorembox}[Invertibility criterion]
A square matrix $A$ is invertible if and only if
\[
\boxed{\det A\neq0}.
\]
If $\det A=0$, the matrix is singular and has no inverse.
\end{theorembox}

\begin{remarkbox}[Proof status]
The criterion is a central theorem connecting determinants, row reduction, and solvability. This course verifies both directions through the behaviour of elimination and through the adjugate identity, but does not present a completely independent proof from the axiomatic construction of the determinant.
\end{remarkbox}

A nonzero determinant means that the matrix has not collapsed independent information. A zero determinant signals structural loss and prevents unique reversal. The practical questions are therefore: what operation does the matrix perform, has information been lost, and if not, which matrix reverses the operation?
