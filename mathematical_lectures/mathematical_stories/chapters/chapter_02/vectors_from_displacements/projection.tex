\subsection{Projection Is Forced by the Right-Angle Condition}

Let $\mathbf v\neq\mathbf0$. We seek a multiple $\lambda\mathbf v$ such that
the remainder
\[
\mathbf u-\lambda\mathbf v
\]
is perpendicular to $\mathbf v$. Geometrically, this means decomposing
$\mathbf u$ into a component parallel to $\mathbf v$ and a component
perpendicular to $\mathbf v$:
\[
\mathbf u
=
\lambda\mathbf v+(\mathbf u-\lambda\mathbf v).
\]

\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=1.0,>=stealth]
    \coordinate (O) at (0,0);
    \coordinate (H) at (3.5,0);
    \coordinate (U) at (3.5,2.5);
    \coordinate (V) at (5.2,0);

    % Direction determined by v
    \draw[helper,->] (O) -- (V)
        node[right] {$\mathbf v$ direction};

    % Original vector u
    \draw[very thick,->] (O) -- (U)
        node[midway,above left] {$\mathbf u$};

    % Parallel component lambda v
    \draw[blue!70!black,line width=1.1pt,->] (O) -- (H)
        node[midway,below=5pt] {$\lambda\mathbf v
        =\operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf v}\mathbf u$};

    % Perpendicular remainder
    \draw[orange!85!black,line width=1.1pt,->] (H) -- (U)
        node[midway,right=5pt] {$\mathbf u-\lambda\mathbf v$};

    % Right-angle marker
    \draw (H) ++(-0.28,0) -- ++(0,0.28) -- ++(0.28,0);

    \fill (O) circle (2pt) node[below left] {$O$};
    \fill (H) circle (2pt) node[below right] {$H$};
    \fill (U) circle (2pt);

    \node[orange!85!black,anchor=west] at (4.0,1.55)
        {$\mathbf u-\lambda\mathbf v\perp\mathbf v$};
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}

The point $H$ is chosen on the line spanned by $\mathbf v$. The vector from
$O$ to $H$ is the parallel component $\lambda\mathbf v$, while the vector from
$H$ to the endpoint of $\mathbf u$ is the perpendicular remainder. The
right-angle condition determines the value of $\lambda$.

The criterion just derived requires
\[
(\mathbf u-\lambda\mathbf v)\cdot\mathbf v=0.
\]
Expanding gives
\[
\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v-
\lambda(\mathbf v\cdot\mathbf v)=0,
\]
so
\[
\lambda
=
\frac{\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v}{\mathbf v\cdot\mathbf v}.
\]
Thus the parallel component is
\[
\operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf v}\mathbf u
=
\frac{\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v}{\mathbf v\cdot\mathbf v}\,\mathbf v.
\]
The formula is the solution of a geometric requirement, not a rule to be
memorised without explanation.
