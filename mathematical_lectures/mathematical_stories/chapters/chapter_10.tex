\noindent\textbf{Limits and continuity.} Sequence limits provide the basic language of convergence. Heine's theorem transfers this language to functions: every admissible approaching sequence must produce the same output limit. Cauchy criteria and existence theorems are deeper structural results; algebraic techniques, one-sided limits, asymptotes, and continuity are applications of that structure rather than the starting point.
