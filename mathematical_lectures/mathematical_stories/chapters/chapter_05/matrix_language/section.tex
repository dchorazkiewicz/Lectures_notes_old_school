\section{Matrices as Structured Information}

\begin{corebox}[The first question is how to read the object]
A matrix is not merely a rectangular table of numbers. It is a structured record
whose rows, columns, and entries have roles determined by the problem. Before
performing any operation, we must identify its shape and interpret what the
positions represent.
\end{corebox}
