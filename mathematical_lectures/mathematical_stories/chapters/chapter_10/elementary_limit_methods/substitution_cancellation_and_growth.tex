\subsection{Substitution, cancellation, and dominant terms}

\begin{corebox}[Use the simplest method that reveals the behavior]
First try direct substitution. If it produces a meaningful value, the calculation is finished. If it produces an indeterminate form such as $0/0$, transform the expression only as much as needed.
\end{corebox}

\begin{examplebox}[Factoring and cancellation]
\[
\lim_{x\to4}\frac{x^2-2x-8}{x^2-9x+20}
=\lim_{x\to4}\frac{(x-4)(x+2)}{(x-4)(x-5)}
=\lim_{x\to4}\frac{x+2}{x-5}=-6.
\]
The cancelled factor records a removable hole, not permission to substitute into the original expression.
\end{examplebox}

\begin{examplebox}[A simple radical]
For
\[
\lim_{x\to0}\frac{\sqrt{1+x}-1}{x},
\]
multiply by the conjugate:
\[
\frac{\sqrt{1+x}-1}{x}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{1+x}+1}{\sqrt{1+x}+1}
=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+x}+1}\to\frac12.
\]
\end{examplebox}

\begin{examplebox}[Behavior at infinity]
\[
\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{3x^2+x}{x^2+4}
=\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{3+1/x}{1+4/x^2}=3.
\]
\end{examplebox}

\begin{remarkbox}
Forms such as $0/0$ are not answers. They indicate that direct substitution has not yet exposed the behavior.
\end{remarkbox}
