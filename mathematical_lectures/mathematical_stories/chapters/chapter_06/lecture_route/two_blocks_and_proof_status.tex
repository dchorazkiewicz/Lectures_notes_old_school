\subsection{Two lecture blocks and the status of the main results}

\begin{corebox}[First 45 minutes: what the determinant detects]
Begin with the $2\times2$ and $3\times3$ formulas, then use simple row and column relations to discover what makes the determinant vanish. The main idea is structural: a zero determinant records loss of independent information.
\end{corebox}

\begin{corebox}[Second 45 minutes: computation without losing meaning]
Minors and cofactors extend the definition, while row operations and triangular form make calculations efficient. Laplace expansion is a general construction, not the preferred method for every matrix.
\end{corebox}

Some general determinant identities are stated because they are needed later. When a full proof is not given, the text says so explicitly and supplies a verification in a simple case or an explanation of the mechanism. Such statements should not be read as if a hidden proof had already been completed.
