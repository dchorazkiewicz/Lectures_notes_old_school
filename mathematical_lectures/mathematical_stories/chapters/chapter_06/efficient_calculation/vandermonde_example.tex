\subsection{A Structured Vandermonde-Type Example}

Consider
\[
V=\begin{pmatrix}
1&1&1\\x&y&z\\x^2&y^2&z^2
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Apply the determinant-preserving column operations
\[
C_2\leftarrow C_2-C_1,
\qquad
C_3\leftarrow C_3-C_1.
\]
Then
\[
\det V=
\det\begin{pmatrix}
1&0&0\\x&y-x&z-x\\x^2&y^2-x^2&z^2-x^2
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Expanding along the first row gives
\[
\det V=
\det\begin{pmatrix}
y-x&z-x\\y^2-x^2&z^2-x^2
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Factoring the columns,
\begin{align*}
\det V
&=(y-x)(z-x)
\det\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\y+x&z+x\end{pmatrix}\\
&=(y-x)(z-x)(z-y).
\end{align*}

\begin{interpretationbox}[The factors expose the zero cases]
The factorization vanishes exactly when two of $x,y,z$ coincide, which is precisely when two columns of the original matrix are equal.
\end{interpretationbox}
