\subsection{A Reliable Calculation Strategy}

\begin{summarybox}[Before computing a determinant]
Check whether rows or columns are equal, proportional, or related by a simple linear combination. Look for zeros and triangular structure. Decide whether row or column operations will simplify the matrix. Track sign changes and extracted factors. Use Laplace expansion only after choosing a useful row or column.
\end{summarybox}

The determinant begins as a definition, but its mathematical value lies in the network of properties that allows the definition to be used intelligently.
