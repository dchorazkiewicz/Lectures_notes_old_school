\subsection{Scaling the Matrix and Multiplying Matrices}

If every row of an $n\times n$ matrix is multiplied by $\lambda$, multilinearity contributes one factor from each row:
\[
\boxed{\det(\lambda A)=\lambda^n\det A}.
\]
This explains why the determinant is not linear as a function of the whole matrix.

\begin{theorembox}[Determinant of a product]
For square matrices of the same size,
\[
\boxed{\det(AB)=\det A\,\det B}.
\]
\end{theorembox}

For diagonal matrices the rule is immediate because both matrix multiplication and the determinant reduce to products of diagonal entries.

\begin{remarkbox}
The product rule concerns ordinary matrix multiplication. It is unrelated to entry-by-entry multiplication and must not be confused with linearity in one row or column.
\end{remarkbox}
