\subsection{Choosing an Expansion Intelligently}

\begin{examplebox}[Use a sparse column]
For
\[
B=\begin{pmatrix}
4&0&1&2\\
0&3&0&-1\\
2&0&5&0\\
1&0&0&6
\end{pmatrix},
\]
the second column contains only one nonzero entry. Therefore
\[
\det B=3C_{22}
=3\det\begin{pmatrix}
4&1&2\\2&5&0\\1&0&6
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
The remaining $3\times3$ determinant is $98$, so
\[
\det B=294.
\]
\end{examplebox}

\begin{corebox}[Before expanding]
Inspect the matrix for zeros, choose a row or column with few nonzero entries, write the cofactor signs explicitly, keep the smaller determinants visible, and combine Laplace expansion with row or column properties when useful.
\end{corebox}
