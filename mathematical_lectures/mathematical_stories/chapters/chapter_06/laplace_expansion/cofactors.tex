\subsection{Cofactors}

The signs in a Laplace expansion alternate in a checkerboard pattern:
\[
\begin{pmatrix}
+&-&+&-\\-&+&-&+\\+&-&+&-\\-&+&-&+
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
The sign in position $(i,j)$ is $(-1)^{i+j}$.

\begin{definitionbox}
The cofactor of $a_{ij}$ is
\[
\boxed{C_{ij}=(-1)^{i+j}M_{ij}}.
\]
\end{definitionbox}

For the preceding example,
\[
M_{12}=-20,
\qquad
C_{12}=(-1)^3M_{12}=20.
\]

\begin{remarkbox}[Minor versus cofactor]
The minor is a determinant of a smaller matrix. The cofactor is that minor together with the sign prescribed by its position.
\end{remarkbox}
