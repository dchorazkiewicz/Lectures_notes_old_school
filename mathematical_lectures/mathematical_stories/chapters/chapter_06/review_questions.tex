\section{Review Exercises}

\subsection{Elementary computational exercises}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Compute
\[
\det\begin{pmatrix}3&-1\\2&4\end{pmatrix}
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\det\begin{pmatrix}5&10\\1&2\end{pmatrix}.
\]
For the second matrix, identify the visible relation between the rows.

\item Compute the determinant of
\[
A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2&0\\-1&3&4\\2&0&5\end{pmatrix}
\]
using the direct $3\times3$ formula.

\item Compute the same determinant as in the previous exercise by expanding along the first row. Compare the amount of arithmetic in the two methods.

\item Evaluate
\[
\det\begin{pmatrix}
2&0&0&0\\
1&-3&0&0\\
4&2&5&0\\
-1&1&3&2
\end{pmatrix}
\]
without Laplace expansion.

\item Let
\[
A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2\\3&4\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Compute the determinant after each operation and compare it with $\det A$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item swap the two rows;
\item multiply the first row by $5$;
\item replace $R_2$ by $R_2-3R_1$.
\end{enumerate}

\item Compute
\[
\det\begin{pmatrix}1&2&3\\0&1&4\\0&0&-2\end{pmatrix}
\]
and then compute the determinant after multiplying the second row by $-3$.

\item Use row operations to simplify and compute
\[
\det\begin{pmatrix}1&2&1\\2&5&3\\3&7&4\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Track every sign change and extracted factor.

\item Expand along a row or column containing the most zeros to compute
\[
\det\begin{pmatrix}
2&0&1&0\\
0&3&0&0\\
4&0&-1&2\\
0&0&0&5
\end{pmatrix}.
\]

\item For
\[
A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2\\0&3\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
B=\begin{pmatrix}2&-1\\4&1\end{pmatrix},
\]
compute $\det A$, $\det B$, $AB$, and $\det(AB)$. Verify the product rule numerically.

\item Determine all values of $t$ for which
\[
\det\begin{pmatrix}t&2\\3&t\end{pmatrix}=0.
\]
For each value, exhibit a nontrivial relation between the two rows or columns.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Conceptual and reasoning exercises}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Explain why the determinant is defined only for square matrices in this course. What structural question is it intended to answer?

\item Interpret $\det A=0$ as loss of information. Give one row-based, one column-based, and one transformation-based description of this loss.

\item Prove directly from the $2\times2$ formula that swapping two rows changes the sign of the determinant.

\item Explain why adding a multiple of one row to another leaves the determinant unchanged. Relate this to the idea that no new independent information is created.

\item A matrix has two proportional rows. Explain, without carrying out a full expansion, why its determinant must be zero.

\item Why is Laplace expansion along any row or column allowed? Explain why the choice affects the work but not the value.

\item Compare the direct $3\times3$ formula, Laplace expansion, triangular structure, and row operations. State when each method is likely to be efficient.

\item Explain geometrically why multiplying one row by a scalar multiplies the determinant by the same scalar.

\item The identity
\[
\det(AB)=\det A\det B
\]
connects composition with scaling. Explain this interpretation without proving the full theorem in arbitrary dimension.

\item Design a reliable strategy for computing a determinant before doing any arithmetic. Your strategy should include checks for zero patterns, proportionality, triangular form, useful row operations, and a sensible expansion choice.
\end{enumerate}
