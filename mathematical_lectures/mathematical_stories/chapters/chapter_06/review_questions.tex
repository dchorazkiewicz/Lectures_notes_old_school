\section{Review Questions}

\begin{enumerate}
\item What kind of object is the determinant? State its domain and codomain, and explain why it is defined only for square matrices.

\item Compute the determinant of a general $2\times2$ matrix and explain geometrically what the sign and absolute value can represent.

\item For a simple $3\times3$ matrix, compute the determinant by one appropriate method. Before calculating, explain why that method is preferable to the alternatives.

\item Explain why equal or proportional rows force the determinant to vanish. What structural loss does this zero record?

\item Describe the effect on the determinant of:
\begin{enumerate}
\item swapping two rows;
\item multiplying one row by a scalar;
\item adding a multiple of one row to another.
\end{enumerate}
Explain why these rules make row reduction useful for determinant calculations.

\item Define a minor and a cofactor. Why does the checkerboard sign pattern appear in Laplace expansion?

\item State Laplace expansion along an arbitrary row or column. What changes when a different row is selected, and what does not change?

\item Explain why a triangular matrix has determinant equal to the product of its diagonal entries. How can row operations be used to exploit this fact?

\item State the product rule $\det(AB)=\det A\det B$ and use it to explain why an invertible matrix must have nonzero determinant.

\item Design a reliable strategy for computing a determinant efficiently. Your answer should describe what to inspect before calculation, how to choose among formulas, expansion, and row operations, and how to verify that the final value is plausible.
\end{enumerate}
