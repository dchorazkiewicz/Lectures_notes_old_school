\subsection{Area Scaling and Collapse in the Plane}

For a $2\times2$ matrix, the absolute determinant measures how the area of the unit square changes. A nonzero determinant produces a genuine parallelogram; a zero determinant makes the two output directions parallel and the area collapses.

\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[>=Latex,scale=0.78]
  \begin{scope}[xshift=-6cm]
    \draw[->] (-0.4,0) -- (3.2,0);
    \draw[->] (0,-0.4) -- (0,3.2);
    \filldraw[fill=gray!12,draw=gray!70] (0,0)--(1,0)--(1,1)--(0,1)--cycle;
    \draw[blue!75!black,very thick,->] (0,0)--(1,0) node[midway,below] {$e_1$};
    \draw[orange!85!black,very thick,->] (0,0)--(0,1) node[midway,left] {$e_2$};
    \node[align=center] at (1.25,-0.9) {unit square\\area $1$};
  \end{scope}

  \begin{scope}[xshift=0cm]
    \draw[->] (-0.4,0) -- (4.2,0);
    \draw[->] (0,-0.4) -- (0,3.7);
    \coordinate (U) at (2,0.5);
    \coordinate (V) at (1,2);
    \filldraw[fill=green!12,draw=green!45!black,thick] (0,0)--(U)--(3,2.5)--(V)--cycle;
    \draw[blue!75!black,very thick,->] (0,0)--(U) node[midway,below right] {$Ae_1$};
    \draw[orange!85!black,very thick,->] (0,0)--(V) node[midway,left] {$Ae_2$};
    \node[align=center] at (1.7,-0.9) {$\det A\ne0$\\area is scaled};
  \end{scope}

  \begin{scope}[xshift=6cm]
    \draw[->] (-0.4,0) -- (4.5,0);
    \draw[->] (0,-0.4) -- (0,4.5);
    \draw[gray!50,line width=3pt] (0,0)--(2.2,4.4);
    \draw[blue!75!black,very thick,->] (0,0)--(1,2) node[midway,left] {$Be_1$};
    \draw[orange!85!black,very thick,->] (0,0)--(2,4) node[midway,right] {$Be_2$};
    \node[align=center] at (2.0,-0.9) {$\det B=0$\\area collapses to a line};
  \end{scope}
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}

\begin{interpretationbox}[What zero means geometrically]
When the column images are independent, they span a parallelogram of nonzero area. When one image is a multiple of the other, every combination remains on one line. The determinant is then zero because a two-dimensional set has been compressed into one dimension.
\end{interpretationbox}
