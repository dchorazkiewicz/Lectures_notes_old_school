\chapter{Cartesian Geometry and Coordinates}

\begin{corebox}[Purpose and learning goals]
This chapter introduces the Cartesian plane as a practical language for describing and investigating geometry. After completing it, the student should be able to:
\begin{itemize}
\item construct and read a Cartesian coordinate system;
\item plot points and elementary graphs, and identify their intersections;
\item translate geometric position and distance into coordinate calculations.
\end{itemize}
The opening Euclidean material is a brief background showing what exists before coordinates; the main subject of the chapter is the Cartesian description.
\end{corebox}
