\chapter{Vectors, Bases, and Coordinate Systems}

\begin{corebox}[Purpose and learning goals]
This chapter turns displacement and direction into algebraic objects. After completing it, the student should be able to:
\begin{itemize}
\item interpret vectors geometrically and perform the basic vector operations;
\item use scalar products, lengths, and simple geometric relations between vectors;
\item describe the same vector in different bases and recognise polar coordinates as another coordinate language.
\end{itemize}
\end{corebox}

\begin{remarkbox}[Lecture emphasis]
The live core is vector displacement, addition, scaling, length, dot product, parallelism, and perpendicularity. Projection, change of basis, and polar coordinates complete the written chapter and may be developed more selectively during the lecture.
\end{remarkbox}
