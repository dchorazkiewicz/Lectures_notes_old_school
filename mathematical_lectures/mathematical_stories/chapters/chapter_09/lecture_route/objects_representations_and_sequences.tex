\subsection{Two lecture blocks: functions first, sequences second}

\begin{corebox}[First 45 minutes: one object, several representations]
A function is an assignment with a domain and codomain, not merely a formula. Tables, graphs, formulas, transformations, compositions, and inverses are different ways of reading the same dependence between quantities.
\end{corebox}

\begin{corebox}[Second 45 minutes: sequences as discrete functions]
A sequence is a function whose inputs are natural numbers. Arithmetic and geometric patterns, recursion, monotonicity, and boundedness prepare the later question of convergence. The calculations stay simple so that discrete structure remains visible.
\end{corebox}

Whenever the text discusses sign, symmetry, monotonicity, periodicity, bounds, or graph transformations, the corresponding picture is part of the mathematical argument rather than an optional illustration.
