\section{Review Exercises}

\subsection{Elementary computational and graphical exercises}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Write a parametric equation of the line through $P=(2,-1)$ with direction $v=(3,4)$. Find the points corresponding to $t=-1,0,2$ and plot them.

\item Find an implicit equation of the line through $P=(1,3)$ with normal vector $n=(2,-1)$. Check that $P$ satisfies the equation.

\item Convert
\[
2x-3y=6
\]
to slope-intercept form, find both axis intercepts, and give one direction vector and one normal vector.

\item Find the line through $A=(-1,2)$ and $B=(3,-4)$ in parametric, implicit, and slope form.

\item Find and plot the intersection of
\[
L_1:y=2x-1,
\qquad
L_2:x+y=5.
\]
Verify the point in both equations.

\item Determine whether each pair is parallel, perpendicular, identical, or neither:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $2x-y=1$ and $4x-2y=7$;
\item $x+2y=3$ and $2x-y=4$;
\item $3x+y=5$ and $6x+2y=10$.
\end{enumerate}

\item Compute the distance from $P=(3,1)$ to the line $2x-y-4=0$. Find the foot of the perpendicular and verify the distance from coordinates.

\item Find the line through $P=(1,-2)$ perpendicular to $3x+2y=5$. Find their intersection.

\item Find the line through $P=(-1,3)$ parallel to $2x-y=4$. Write it in implicit and parametric form.

\item A line has direction vector $(2,-3)$ and passes through $(4,1)$. Find its intersections with both coordinate axes and draw the line.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Development and reasoning exercises}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Write a parametric equation of the plane through $P=(1,2,0)$ with directions $u=(1,0,1)$ and $v=(0,2,-1)$. Produce four points of the plane and explain why the nonparallel directions generate a two-dimensional set.

\item For the same plane, compute a normal vector using $u\times v$ and derive an implicit equation. Verify that the points found previously satisfy it.

\item Find the plane through
\[
P=(1,0,0),\qquad Q=(0,1,0),\qquad R=(0,0,1).
\]
Give both parametric and normal forms and explain how the three-point problem reduces to two direction vectors.

\item Determine the relative position of
\[
\Pi_1:x+y+z=1,
\qquad
\Pi_2:x-y+z=3.
\]
Find a direction vector of their intersection line and one point on it, then write the line parametrically.

\item Compare the line formula
\[
P=P_0+t v
\]
with the plane formula
\[
P=P_0+s u+t v.
\]
Use one concrete line and one concrete plane to show that the number of independent directions determines the geometric dimension.

\item Explain why a normal equation describes a line in the plane and a plane in space by the same idea: the displacement from a fixed point has zero normal component. Give both formulas side by side.

\item A student converts every line immediately to slope form. Give two problems in which parametric or normal form is more natural, solve them briefly, and explain what slope form hides.

\item Give a complete strategy for choosing a representation of a line or plane. Your answer must discuss constructing the object, testing membership, finding intersections, checking parallelism or perpendicularity, and measuring distance.
\end{enumerate}
