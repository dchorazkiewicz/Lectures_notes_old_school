\section{Review Questions}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Explain why a line is determined by one point and one nonzero direction. Why is a plane determined by one point and two independent directions?

\item Write the parametric equation of the line through $P=(1,2)$ with direction $v=(3,-1)$. Explain what the parameter means geometrically.

\item Find a parametric description of the line through two distinct points $P$ and $Q$. Why does this description also work for vertical lines?

\item Compare parametric, slope, and implicit equations of a line. For each form, name one question that it answers especially efficiently.

\item Given the line $2x-3y=6$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item identify a normal vector;
\item find a direction vector;
\item determine its intersections with the coordinate axes;
\item explain how these pieces of information are encoded in the same equation.
\end{enumerate}

\item State and justify tests for parallelism and perpendicularity using direction vectors and normal vectors. How do these tests relate to slope formulas in the nonvertical case?

\item Explain the general principle used to find an intersection of geometric sets. Apply it to two lines and describe the possible outcomes.

\item Derive the normal equation of a plane from a point $P_0$ and a normal vector $n$. What geometric condition is imposed on every displacement $P-P_0$ lying in the plane?

\item Compare the parametric and normal descriptions of a plane. Which data are fixed in each description, and which questions are easier in one form than the other?

\item A formula may fail to cover a special coordinate case even when the geometric object remains ordinary. Explain this statement using vertical lines and then give an analogous example involving planes or lines in space.
\end{enumerate}
