\chapter{Integrals and Accumulation}

\begin{corebox}[Purpose and learning goals]
This chapter connects antiderivatives with accumulated change. After completing it, the student should be able to:
\begin{itemize}
\item find elementary antiderivatives and use simple substitution or integration by parts;
\item interpret definite integrals through Riemann sums and the fundamental theorem of calculus;
\item model area, average value, displacement, work, volume, and total change as accumulated local contributions.
\end{itemize}
\end{corebox}

\begin{summarybox}[Integration as accumulated change]
Antiderivatives reverse local differentiation. Riemann sums explain how totals arise from small contributions, and the fundamental theorem is the central bridge connecting those two ideas. Area, average value, volume, displacement, distance, work, and total change are applications of one principle: integrate a local contribution over the interval or region where it acts.
\end{summarybox}
